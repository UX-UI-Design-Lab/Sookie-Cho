About me

I am an interdisciplinary researcher and intergrated designer across academia and industry with decades of experience in scientific design research methods, design process, and interactive media storytelling. I conducted various experience design and research projects in Samsung Electronics, Art Center NABi, and SK Telecom for next-generation products and services by envisioning scenarios, innovative product design prototypes, usability/user testing, strategic plans, and design process modeling and management.

Design, in my perspective, is a decision-making process for positive changes in human well-being. We stand as designers who are responsible for the world with the artifacts we’ve made, as well as decision-makers who can introduce creative solutions that can help other people for better choices. For better decision-making, designers should understand humans in terms of perception, cognition, emotion, judgment, and interplay within them. Designers need to have a scientific mind: observation, pattern-finding, modeling, and evaluation with feedback in order to predict the effect of the designs. We can expand the understanding of design through theories and methods from humane studies.

I’m currently an independent design researcher and expanding my research area to Digital Humanities for Art/Design History, Aesthetics, and Psychology. I taught Cognitive/Social Psychology, Visual/Interaction/Experience Design, Non-Linear Interactive Storytelling, and Creative Coding at the academic institutions including Yonsei University, the University of Michigan, Northern Kentucky University, the University of Cincinnati, and currently teaching at UCLA. Since 2020, I co-founded HUE Project (Humans in User Experience) and serve as an advisor and principal project manager to advocate human-centered design and research. UX research + Web
Chef-farid + HUE
I was introduced to Chef Farid who was a multicultural chef and wanted to open his chef business. To help him with the web presence, HUE conducted user research and developed the user experience design with intensive benchmarking, co-creative map, demand analysis, semistructured interview, persona-scenarios, wireframes, interface design system, impression test, and perception studies with multivariate analysis through Amazon Mturk.

Designing Accessibility
SLSA @Purdue
In this presentation, I claim that how we design an 'abstraction' process of the content matters for accessible and approachable online reading. I review the design theories and practices of abstraction and suggest Compression, Translation, and Association as conceptual mechanisms of folding and unfolding the contents.

Taking a researcher-introspective approach, we reflectively analyzed the processes and outcomes of the inquiry. The findings revealed that during the creative practice, the activity of seeing is a meaning-making process that is guided, shifted, and diffracted through visual conversations between visible and invisible qualities suggested by compositional rules and computer vision.
